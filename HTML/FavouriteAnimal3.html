Hippopotamus

Hippopotamus amphibius

This is a hippo running in the wild. Hippos generally live in East Africa, and tend to live in lakes, rivers, and swamps. Hippos eat aquatic plants, like short green shoots and reeds, and creeping grasses.

This is a hippo swimming in the water. Hippos are the third largest living land animals. Hippos spend alot of time in the water, since it can cool them down in the hot swamps of Sub-Sahara Africa.

This is a hippo swimming underwater. Hippos can hold their breaths underwater for 5 minutes, then they have to come back up for air. Hippos bob up and down and it allows them to sleep underwater.



Eating Habits of the Hippopotamus

Hippos are omnivorous animals, since they eat meat and plants. Hippos do not eat when they are in the water, and tend to eat short grasses found on land. Hippos spend about 5 or more hours every evening to find food to eat. There are some reports about hippos practicing cannibalism when food is scarce.
