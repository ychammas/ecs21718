Rosa Parks

Girl on the Bus

Rosa Parks protested segregation when she refused to give up her seat in the "coloured" section of the bus to a white woman when the "white" section was filled.
Her act of defiance led to the Montgomery bus boycott, which was an important part of the Civil Rights Movement.
After being fired from her job, she moved to Detroit, and was a secretary and receptionist to John Coyners.
